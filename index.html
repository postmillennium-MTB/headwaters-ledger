Data notes & confidence

Solid: compact parties, dates, and allocation terms come from compact texts, state engineer offices, and the Bureau of Reclamation and State Department dockets. Colorado River 2026 process dates from Reclamation's Post-2026 NEPA docket. Columbia River Treaty status from the U.S. State Department and Province of B.C.

Estimates (†): flow averages, aquifer-volume comparisons, and "longest undammed river" style superlatives are widely cited figures that vary by source and measurement method. Percentage splits within tributary compacts are summarized, not quoted. The Ledger Balance pie charts are rough proportions assembled from state and provincial water plans plus long-term gauge averages, rounded to whole percents — reliable as shares, not as gauge readings, and annual totals swing enormously between wet and dry years.

Not legal advice: built by a trail advocate, not a water attorney. The Law of the River keeps actual water attorneys extremely busy.
